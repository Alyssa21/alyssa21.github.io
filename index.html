What are dogs Well, you should know what a dog is, but just in case you don't, here is a scientific description of a dog; The domestic dog is a member of genus Canis that forms part of the wolf-like canids, and is the most widely abundant carnivore. Scientific name: Canis lupus familiaris Gestation period: 58 – 68 days Lifespan: 10 – 13 years Height: 6.3 – 110 cm (At Shoulder) Daily sleep: 12 – 14 hours (Adult) Where, when and how did dogs originate? The many different breeds of dog we know today all originated from the wolf When, how and why were dogs domesticated? Many people think dogs were first domesticated in Europe more than 15,000 years ago. Opposing researchers believe the domestication happened approximately 12,500 years ago in Central Asia or China. What jobs dogs used for now? Sheep dogs, guide dogs, police dogs, breeding, mail/customs dogs, therapy dogs, racing dogs, hunting, search and rescue dogs, acting dogs, sled dogs, dog fighting, police dogs, pets, Dog breeding, and many more. Why are certain breeds used for certain jobs? !
